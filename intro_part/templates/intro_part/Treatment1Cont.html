The whole experiment contains 40 rounds, equally divided into 4 Blocks. The value of Q remains constant within each Block, but may vary between Blocks. In the beginning of each Block, we will show you a table with information that applies to the following Block.

We will first show you a practice round, Round 0, which will not count towards your monetary payoff. The table below means in Round 0, Q=8 with probability 50%, and Q=5 with probability 50%. In other words, they are equally likely. In addition, out of the 5 urns with higher Q, 2 are red and 3 are black; out of the 5 urns with lower Q 2 are red and 3 are black. The number of red urns in high-Q urns and the number of red urns in low-Q urns are always equal.
